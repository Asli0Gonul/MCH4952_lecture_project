Careers at DeepDive

We are building a focused, creative, and engineering-driven environment for people who want to make underwater robotics easier to learn, design, and explore.

Our Working Environment

DeepDive Technologies is designed as a hands-on product and learning studio. The work environment is collaborative, fast-moving, and project-based. Team members are encouraged to test ideas quickly, build small prototypes, improve the user experience, and turn complex underwater robotics concepts into simple digital tools.

We value curiosity, clear communication, visual thinking, and practical engineering. Whether someone works on frontend design, technical content, robotics research, or videos, the goal is the same: make underwater robotics more understandable and accessible for students and early-stage engineers.

Open Positions

Frontend Developer Intern

Who we are looking for: A student or junior developer who enjoys clean layouts, responsive design, and interactive web tools.

What you will do: Build calculator interfaces, improve page transitions, create reusable components, and make the platform easier to use on mobile and desktop.

Skills: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, basic Git/GitHub, attention to detail.

Robotics Content Research Intern

Who we are looking for: Someone interested in underwater robotics, ROV/AUV systems, and technical research.

What you will do: Summarize learning topics, prepare short explanations, collect useful references, and organize content into beginner-friendly learning paths.

Skills: Research ability, technical writing, robotics curiosity, English reading/writing.

UI/UX Designer

Who we are looking for: A visual thinker who can turn engineering content into a clear, modern, and easy-to-navigate interface.

What you will do: Design cards, page layouts, platform flow, icons, and visual hierarchy for tools, videos, careers, and learning sections.

Skills: Figma or similar tools, user empathy, layout design, consistency.

Marine Robotics Research Assistant

Who we are looking for: A motivated student who wants to connect engineering theory with ROV/AUV applications.

What you will do: Support tool logic, collect example calculations, review ROV components, and help prepare video/resource recommendations.

Skills: Mechatronics, mechanical design, electronics, control basics, documentation.

Video Content Curator

Who we are looking for: Someone who can find useful ROV videos and group them by learning value.

What you will do: Curate tutorial videos, add short descriptions, classify topics, and keep the video library relevant.

Skills: Content organization, technical understanding, communication.

ROV Tool Development Assistant

Who we are looking for: A practical engineering student who likes calculators and early-stage design decisions.

What you will do: Improve buoyancy, battery, thrust, and component selection tools with clearer formulas and examples.

Skills: Physics fundamentals, Excel/JS basics, engineering calculation logic.
